    Caroline Myers

    Vision science: how perception stays stable when the world moves.

    Ph.D. candidate at Johns Hopkins University studying visual perception and attention with psychophysics. I’m especially interested in adaptation and reference frames (retinotopic vs. object‑based/spatiotopic), and what those constraints reveal about the format of perceptual representations.

    Current focus
    Object‑based adaptation • Spatiotopic stability • Visual number & feature processing
    Recent work
    Current Biology (2025) • iScience (2025) • Nature Reviews Psychology (2022)
